## How AI Is Used in Trialsplained

Trialsplained uses AI to rewrite official clinical trial descriptions — sourced from ClinicalTrials.gov and the NIHR Clinical Research Network — into plain English summaries.

This document explains what AI does in this product, what it cannot do, where it is likely to fail, and what safeguards are in place.

---

## What the AI Does

1. **Receives** structured trial data from public registries (title, phase, eligibility criteria, intervention description, status, location, commitment requirements)
2. **Rewrites** this data in plain English, targeting approximately a Year 9 reading level
3. **Generates** a short summary, a "what would joining involve?" section, and key info fields
4. **Does not** access any patient data, recommend trials to individuals, or generate information beyond what is in the source registry record

---

## Known Limitations

| Limitation | Description | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Hallucination | AI may generate plausible-sounding information that is not in the source data | Last-verified date on every card; link to official listing; feedback button |
| Oversimplification | Plain English rewrites may lose important nuance, particularly around risk | Risk information is explicitly preserved in prompts; feedback mechanism exists |
| Currency | Trial status changes; AI summary may not reflect the current state | Last-verified date shown; users directed to official listing to verify status |
| Bias | Training data may underrepresent certain conditions, populations, or geographies | Acknowledged limitation; future audit planned |
| Language | Summaries are in English only | Known gap; translation planned for future version |
| Eligibility | AI cannot determine whether a specific person is eligible for a trial | Eligibility criteria shown; users directed to speak to their doctor |

---

## What the AI Will Never Do in This Product

- Assess whether a specific user is suitable for a trial
- Recommend a trial to a user based on their profile
- Generate information that is not sourced from the official registry record
- Claim that any treatment is safe or effective
- Replace a conversation with a clinical professional

---

## Human Oversight Model

Trialsplained is currently a single-person project. The current oversight model is:

- All AI-generated summaries include a last-verified date
- A visible feedback mechanism ("Is this accurate?") is present on every trial card
- Feedback to feedback@trialsplained.com is reviewed manually
- Summaries flagged as inaccurate are reviewed and corrected within 48 hours
- The official registry listing is always one click away

This is a minimum viable oversight model appropriate for a zero-revenue early-stage product. As usage scales, a more formal review process will be required.

---

## Model Transparency

- **Model used:** AI language model (via Lovable)
- **Model version pinning:** Not currently implemented — a future version will pin to a specific model version for consistency
- **Prompt engineering:** Summaries are generated with explicit instructions to preserve risk information, disclose placebo arms, and avoid promotional language
- **No fine-tuning:** The base model is used without domain-specific fine-tuning at this stage

---

## AI Label

Every AI-generated summary in Trialsplained includes this visible label:

> *"Plain English summary generated by AI · Last verified [date] · View official listing →"*

This label is not hidden, not in small print, and cannot be toggled off.

---

## Relationship to Regulatory Guidance

The use of AI to rewrite public information for accessibility purposes is not currently classified as a medical device function under UKCA, CE, or FDA frameworks. However, this assessment may change as the product develops. See `REGULATORY_FRAMEWORK.md`.
